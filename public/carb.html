Flapjacks

Nutrition
  • (Serves 12)
  • Per serving:
  • Calories: 139
  • Fat: 5.8g
  • Carbs: 18.6g
  • Protein: 2.8g

  • 1 large egg
  • 50g oats
  • 30g protein powder
  • 100ml skim milk
  • 1/4 tsp backing powder
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract

  1. Add oats to blender and blend until fine.
  2. Add rest of ingredients to blender and blend until smooth.
  3. Spray a non-stick pan with low calorie cooking spray and place over a medium heat.
  4. Pour the mixture into the middle of the pan (makes about 6 small pancakes). Cook on one side for about 1 minute until golden brown then flip over and cook the other side.
  5. Serve the pancakes with your favourite fresh fruit and toppings!

  • 50g wholemeal flour
  • 175g porridge oats
  • 1 beaten egg
  • 50g coconut oil
  • 1tbsp honey (or maple syrup)
  • 1 ripened, mashed banana
  • 2 drops vanilla extract
  • 50g raisins (or any dried fruit)

    1. Preheat the oven to 180degrees Celsius.
    2. Add all the dry ingredients to a bowl.
    3. Melt the coconut oil in the pan.
    4. Add the coconut oil into the bowl of dry ingredients along with the honey, beaten egg, mashed banana and raisins.
    5. Line a baking tray with parchment paper and spoon the mixture into the tray.
    6. Bake for approximately 25 minutes, until golden brown.
    7. Allow to cool before slicing into 12 slices.

    • (Serves 1)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 294
    • Fat: 7.3g
    • Carbs: 32g

    • 1 tsp paprika
    • 3 chicken breasts, sliced
    • Juice of ½ a lemon
    • 2 400g tins of chopped tomatoes
    • 150ml water
    • 1 tsp sugar
    • 2 cloves of garlic crushed
    • 2 tsp of chopped fresh rosemary
    • 175g sliced chorizo
    • Pinch of dried chilli flakes
    • 100ml fresh cream (2-3 tablespoons)
    • 150g baby spinach leaves
    • 2 tbsp fresh chopped parsley
    • 400g penne pasta

    1. Marinate the chicken with the paprika, lemon juice and I tbsp olive oil. Leave aside.
    2. Spray some low calorie oil on a pan and heat over medium heat. Brown the chicken pieces in the pan, then remove from to a plate. In the same pan, lightly fry the sliced chorizo on both sides.
    3. Add the garlic and rosemary to the pan, ensuring the garlic doesn’t burn. Then add the chopped tomatoes, water, sugar, chilli flakes and chopped parsley. Season with salt and pepper. Add the chicken back into the sauce.
    4. Bring to the boil and then turn down to a low heat and simmer for 15 minutes. Add the cream and simmer for another 2 to 3 minutes.
    5. Meanwhile, cook the pasta.
    6. Add the pasta and the spinach to the cooking sauce and cook for a further minute or two.
    7. Serve immediately.

  • 1 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1 tbsp minced ginger
  • 1 tbsp crushed garlic
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 tbsp mild curry powder
  • 1 tsp dried coriander

  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • 3 medium sweet potatoes
  • 65g tomato puree
  • 1 400g tin of chopped tomato

  • 240ml vegetable stock
  • 1 400ml tin of coconut milk
  • 180g of broccoli
  • 180g cauliflower
  • 3 small sliced courgette
  • 90g baby spinach

  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 200g basmati rice to serve

    1. Add the oil to a large pan and place on medium heat. Add the ginger, garlic, onion, curry powder, coriander, turmeric and cayenne pepper. Sauté until the onions have softened.
    2. Add the chopped sweet potatoes, tomato paste, tin of tomatoes, vegetable stock and coconut milk.
    3. Allow to simmer and place the lid on the pot, leave to simmer until the potatoes have slightly softened.
    4. Add the broccoli, cauliflower and courgette to the pan. Simmer until all the vegetables are al dente. Add in the baby spinach and sugar, simmer until the spinach has wilted.
    5. Serve with the basmati rice.

    Chilli Con Carne

    Bolognese

    Nutrition
    • (Serves 4)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 493
    • Fat: 6.5g
    • Carbs: 108.1g
    • Protein: 43.7g
    Nutrition
    • (Serves 6)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 412
    • Fat: 6.9g
    • Carbs: 56.6g
    • Protein: 32.1g

    • 1 large onion diced
    • 1 chopped red pepper
    • 2 crushed garlic cloves
    • 1 heaped tsp hot chilli powder (or 1 level tbsp of mild chilli powder)
    • 1 tsp paprika
    • 1 tsp ground cumin
    • 1 beef stock cube
    • 400g can chopped tomatoes
    • 1 tsp sugar
    • 2 tbsp tomato puree
    • 500g lean minced beef
    • 410g can red kidney beans
    • 200g long grained rice to sere

    1. Place your pan over medium heat and spray some low calorie oil into the pan. Allow to heat.
    2. Add the onion to the pan and cook over medium heat until soft (approximately 5 minutes), stirring occasionally. Add the garlic, pepper, chilli powder, paprika and cumin into the pan. Mix until all combined and cook for a further 5 minutes.
    3. Remove the mix from the pan and into a bowl, and set aside. Brown the mince by adding it into the now empty pan and turn up the heat. Break up the meat with the spoon, ensuring the pan is hot enough for the meat to brown. Continue browning the meat until none of the meat is pink.
    4. Once the meat is cooked, add the mix of veg and spices back into the meat, and mix to combine.
    5. Meanwhile add 300ml to the stock cube and dissolve it before adding it to the pan. Add the can of chopped tomatoes and sugar. Season with salt and pepper. Add the tomato puree and stir the sauce.
    6. Bring the sauce to the boil, before stirring the sauce and placing the lid on the pan. Turn down the heat until it is gently simmering and leave to simmer for 20 minutes.
    7. If the sauce begins to dry out, you can add a couple of tablespoons of water to the sauce.
    8. Drain and rinse the kidney beans in a sieve and stir them into the chilli pot. Bring to the boil once more, without the lid for a further 10 minutes.
    9. Season generously to taste. Turn off the heat and leave to stand for 10 mins.
    10. Serve with rice.

  • 500g minced beef
  • Approx 80g of lardons
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 2 400ml tins of tomato
  • 2 tbsp of tomato puree
  • 8 tbsp red wine
  • Fresh basil
  • 400g wholegrain pasta of your choice

    1. Heat a large pan over medium heat and spray a generous amount of low calorie cooking oil in the pan. Gently soften the diced onion, crushed garlic and lardons in the pan for approx. five minutes.
    2. Remove the onion, garlic and lardons from the pan and into a bowl. Then brown the beef in the same pan, stirring continuously.
    3. Add the onion, garlic and lardons back into the pan. Now add the tins of tomato, tomato puree, wine, basil and season with salt and pepper.
    4. Put the lid on and simmer for 20 minutes, then gently simmer without the lid for 20-25 minutes.
    5. Meanwhile cook the pasta and serve.

    • 1lb extra lean ground beef
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 4 burger buns
    • 1 tomato sliced
    • 8 leaves of bibb lettuce
    • 2tbsp ketchup
    • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
    • For the Black pepper Marinade:
    • 2 tbsp whole coriander seeds
    • 2 tbsp whole black peppercorns
    • ½ tbsp whole cumin seeds
    • 6 cloves garlic, peeled
    • 1 inch of fresh ginger, sliced
    • ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil
    • ¼ cup brown sugar

    1. To make the black pepper marinade, grind the coriander seeds, peppercorns and cumin seeds in a spice grinder until very fine. Add the remaining marinade ingredients to a food processor and blend until smoot. Add the ground spices and blend once more.
    2. In a mixing bowl combine the beef, 3 tbsp of the marinade (store the rest in the fridge for up to a week), and salt for 10 minutes.
    3. Heat the grill pan to medium heat. Form the beef into 4 burgers and grill for 2-3 minutes on each side or until completely cooked through.
    4. Spread ½ tbsp ketchup and ½ tbsp mustard on each bun, place a burger in each bun and then add the tomato and the lettuce.

  • 1 cup plain flour
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp baking soda
  • ½ cup peanut butter
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup light brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp cold water
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ cup chopped nuts

    1. Preheat the oven to 375deg/f. Line the baking sheets with parchment paper.
    2. Combine the flour, salt and baking soda in a bowl.
    3. Beat the nut butter until smooth, mix in the sugars, the egg, water and vanilla. Mix until thick and glossy.
    4. Add the flour and mix until combined, add more water if needed. Add in the nuts and fold to combine.
    5. Separate the dough into two tablespoon portions and place onto the baking parchment. Bake for 10-11 minutes until golden brown.
    6. Place on a wire rack once baked and allow to cool.

    • Carbs: 48g
    • Protein: 12g

  • 1 large sweet potato (about 400g)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper/li>
  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • ¼ teaspoon paprika

    1. Preheat oven to 200 degrees Celsius.
    2. Slice up sweet potato into fries.
    3. Add sliced sweet potato, oil, salt, pepper and paprika to a large bowl and toss until evenly coated.
    4. Line a baking tray with parchment paper.
    5. Spread out fries on tray and bake for 35 minutes.

    • 3 tbsp olive oil
    • 2 sweet potatoes
    • 2 tsp smokes paprika
    • 2 tsp ground cumin
    • 1 onion
    • 2 carrots
    • 2 garlic cloves
    • 2 celery sticks
    • 1 tsp dried oregano
    • 2 tsp chilli powder
    • 1 tbsp tomato puree
    • 1 red pepper
    • 400g black beans
    • 400g kidney beans
    • 800g chopped tomatoes

    1. Peel and cut sweet potato into medium-sized chunks, evenly coat with 1 ½ tbsp oil, 1 tsp smoked paprika and 1 tsp ground cumin.
    2. Season with salt and pepper and roast for 25 minutes.
    3. Meanwhile, chop the onion, carrots and celery and cook for 8-10 minutes with the remaining oil in a large saucepan over a medium heat. After, crush the garlic and add to the saucepan, cook for a further minute.
    4. Mix in the remaining dry spices and tomato puree into the vegetables and cook for one more minute.
    5. Add in the red pepper, chopped into small pieces, along with the chopped tomatoes and 200ml water. Bring to the boil then allow simmer for 20 minutes.
    6. Add the beans and cook for another 10 minutes.
    7. Add in the roasted sweet potato and serve with guacamole, rice, coriander and lime.

    4. Add in all remaining ingredients and cook on a low heat until everything is fully heated.
