About protein

  • A high-protein diet is beneficial for promoting weight loss. A high intake of protein reduces appetite and keeps you fuller for longer by increasing levels of appetite-reducing hormones, while reducing levels of the hunger hormone, ghrelin. This can help lead to a reduction in overall calorie consumption, and therefore aids weight loss. A high-protein diet is beneficial for promoting weight loss. A high intake of protein reduces appetite and keeps you fuller for longer by increasing levels of appetite-reducing hormones, while reducing levels of the hunger hormone, ghrelin. This can help lead to a reduction in overall calorie consumption, and therefore aids weight loss.

  • Protein also helps fat loss by increasing metabolism. One way in which a protein-rich diet can aid metabolism is along with strength training, a high-protein intake can help build muscle. This increases metabolism as muscle burns more calories than fat.

  • Another way in which protein can increase metabolism is that protein has a higher thermic effect of food than other macro-nutrients. This refers to the number of calories needed to digest and metabolize it. Roughly 15-30% of protein calories are burned during digestion compared to only 0-3% of calories from fat and 5-10% of calories coming from carbohydrates. Consuming roughly 30% of your daily calories from protein optimizes weight loss by boosting your metabolism and causing a reduction in calorie intake.

  • Protein is essential for growth and repair of muscles. Research has shown that protein powder increases the levels of amino acids in the bloodstream, which in turn increases the muscle synthesis response post workout. Whey protein has shown to be particularly effective at promoting muscle growth when consumed right before, during or just after strength training. Consuming 2g of protein per kg of lean body mass has been shown to maximise results from strength-training and promote muscle growth.

  • Whey protein comes from dairy. It contains all the essential amino acids. Casein protein is also dairy based with all essential amino acids. It is slowly absorbed in comparison to whey protein which releases amino acids quicker. Whey protein has been shown to be more effective for muscle growth, while casein protein is helpful to prevent muscle breakdown. For optimal results it is best to use a combination of both, whey protein around workouts and casein protein at night before you go to bed.

  • Isolate proteins are further processed to remove more fat and lactose and they contain a higher percentage of protein than standard protein powders.

  • Plant based protein powders include soy protein and pea protein. Soy protein is plant-based, and it contains all the essential amino acids. Pea protein is also fully plant-based however, it is low in the amino acids cystine and methionine and does not provide the same amount of each amino acids that animal-based protein powders contain.
