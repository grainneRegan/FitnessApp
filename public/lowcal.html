Fishcakes

Nutrition
  • (Serves 6)
  • Per serving:
  • Calories: 336
  • Fat: 8.7g
  • Carbs: 28g
  • Protein: 20.3g
Nutrition
  • (Serves 6)
  • Per serving:
  • Calories: 153
  • Fat: 7g
  • Carbs: 5g
  • Protein: 16g

  • 500g lean minced beef
  • 2 celery sticks
  • 2 onions
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 150ml red wine
  • 400g chopped tomatoes
  • 2 large carrots
  • 400ml beef stock
  • 550 ml low fat milk
  • 3 tbsp cornflour
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 large courgettes
  • 4 vine tomatoes
  • 20g parmesan
  • 40g vintage cheddar
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg

  1. Preheat the oven 180 degrees Celsius.
  2. Fry mince over medium heat for 5 minutes, until golden brown. Finely chop the onions and celery.
  3. Fry half the onions and the celery over a medium heat for 5-6 minutes and season well. Crush the garlic, add to the onions and celery and fry for a further minute. Grate the carrots and add in, along with the red wine. Simmer for 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add the tinned tomatoes, stock and bring to the boil. Cover with lid and turn down heat and let simmer for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove lid and simmer for a further 15 minutes or until sauce has thickened.
  5. Meanwhile, put remaining onions in a separate pan along with the peppercorns and milk (reserving 3 tbsp) and cook over a low heat. Simmer for 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool for 10 minutes.
  6. After cooled, drain with a sieve.
  7. Put cornflour and reserved milk in a small bowl and whisk until a thick paste is formed. Then add to the strained milk and simmer over low heat for 5 minutes. Add the mustard and ground nutmeg and season with salt and pepper
  8. Cut courgette into thin slices lengthways. Blanch in hot water for 1 minute.
  9. Spread half the mince mixture out in a lasagne dish. Layer half the courgette slices on top.
  10. Repeat step 9.
  11. Pour white sauce over top. Slice tomatoes and place on top of white sauce. Grate the cheddar and parmesan cheese and sprinkle on top.
  12. Bake for 30 minutes.
  13. Cut into six slices and serve with a side salad.

  • 450g cod
  • 35g coconut flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 eggs
  • Handful parsley, chopped
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper

    1. Season cod with salt and pepper.
    2. Fry fish with a little oil, over a medium heat for 10 minutes.
    3. After cooked, use a fork to break cod up into small pieces.
    4. Add in the parsley, onions, flour and eggs along with the spices.
    5. Coarsely blend mixture in a food processor.
    6. Compress mixture into fishcakes.
    7. With the remaining oil, fry fish cakes over a medium heat for about 3 minutes on each side.
    8. Serve with a side salad.

  • 50g wholemeal flour

  • 1 beaten egg
  • 50g coconut oil
  • 1tbsp honey (or maple syrup)
  • 1 ripened, mashed banana
  • 2 drops vanilla extract
  • 50g raisins (or any dried fruit)

    2. Add all the dry ingredients to a bowl.

    Chia Pudding

    Nutrition
    • (Serves 4)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 367
    • Fat: 10g
    • Carbs: 48g
    • Protein: 12g
    Nutrition
    • (Serves 1)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 241
    • Fat: 14.1g
    • Carbs: 21.3g
    • Protein: 8.4g

    • 3 tbsp olive oil
    • 2 sweet potatoes
    • 2 tsp smokes paprika
    • 2 tsp ground cumin
    • 1 onion
    • 2 carrots
    • 2 garlic cloves
    • 2 celery sticks
    • 1 tsp dried oregano
    • 2 tsp chilli powder
    • 1 tbsp tomato puree
    • 1 red pepper
    • 400g black beans
    • 400g kidney beans
    • 800g chopped tomatoes

    1. Peel and cut sweet potato into medium-sized chunks, evenly coat with 1 ½ tbsp oil, 1 tsp smoked paprika and 1 tsp ground cumin.
    2. Season with salt and pepper and roast for 25 minutes.
    3. Meanwhile, chop the onion, carrots and celery and cook for 8-10 minutes with the remaining oil in a large saucepan over a medium heat. After, crush the garlic and add to the saucepan, cook for a further minute.
    4. Mix in the remaining dry spices and tomato puree into the vegetables and cook for one more minute.
    5. Add in the red pepper, chopped into small pieces, along with the chopped tomatoes and 200ml water. Bring to the boil then allow simmer for 20 minutes.
    6. Add the beans and cook for another 10 minutes.
    7. Add in the roasted sweet potato and serve with guacamole, rice, coriander and lime.

  • 3 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 tbsp sweetener of choice
  • 230 ml almond milk
  • ¼ teaspoon vanilla essence

    1. Combine all ingredients in a mason jar or small bowl.
    2. Let mixture sit for 5 minutes, then stir again to break up any large clumps of chia seeds.
    3. Cover mixture and refrigerate for 1-2 hours or leave overnight.
    4. Serve with your favourite toppings such as fresh fruit and granola.

    Chicken and Chorizo Rice bake

    Overnight Oats

    Nutrition
    • (Serves 6)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 334
    • Fat: 11.8g
    • Carbs: 16g
    • Protein: 41.3g
    Nutrition
    • (Serves 2)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 270
    • Fat: 6g
    • Carbs: 44g
    • Protein: 12g

    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • Knob of butter
    • 6 chicken breasts or thighs
    • 1 onion chopped
    • 3 garlic cloves chopped
    • 1 celery stalk, chopped
    • 100g chorizo, sliced
    • 350g rice, basmati
    • 100ml white wine
    • 800ml chicken stock
    • 1 sprig thyme and 1 bay leaf
    • 2 tbsp fresh parsley

    1. Preheat the oven to 180 deg C
    2. Heat a casserole dish on the hob, add 1 tbsp of the oil and butter, and brown the seasoned chicken pieces on both sides for 3-4 minutes, then remove to a dish.
    3. Add more of the oil, then add the onion, garlic and celery and sauté until softened.
    4. Add the chorizo, then the rice and a pinch of salt and combine with the vegetables.
    5. Add the wine, stirring to combine, then the bay-leaf and thyme.
    6. Add the stock, and them place the chicken back into the casserole on top of the rice.
    7. Put the lid on and place in the oven for about 30-40 minutes until the chicken and rice is cooked and the stock has been absorbed.
    8. Check after about 20 minutes, add more stock if needed.
    9. Check seasoning before serving.

  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 230 ml almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla essence

  • 80g oats
  • 140g non-fat Greek yoghurt

    1. Combine all ingredients in a large class container.
    2. Cover mixture and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or leave overnight.
    3. Serve with your favourite toppings such as fresh fruit and granola.

    • Fat: 3g

    • 2 lemons
    • 1 ½ tablespoons fresh oregano
    • 1 ½ tbsp sunflower oil
    • 2 tsp crushed garlic
    • 4 chicken breasts
    • 225g thin asparagus
    • 8 spring onions
    • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
    • 1 ½ tbsp white wine vinegar
    • 140g rocket

    1. Grate the two lemons . Add the lemon zest, oregano, sunflower oil and garlic in a large bowl. Add the chicken into the bowl and toss to coat.
    2. Spray the pan with low calorie cooking spray and place over medium heat. Season the chicken with salt and pepper. Add the chicken to the pan, cook for 5 minutes each side. Half both the lemons and place cut side down in the pan, cook for 4 minutes. Remove both the chicken and lemon form pan once cooked, slice the chicken.
    3. Add the asparagus and the onions to the pan. Cook until tender, turning to ensure that they are evenly cooked. Cut both the onions and asparagus.
    4. Pour the mixture into the middle of the pan (makes about 6 small pancakes). Cook on one side for about 1 minute until golden brown then flip over and cook the other side.
    5. Add the olive oil, vinegar and some salt and pepper into a bowl. Add the asparagus, onions and rocket into the bowl. Serve with the chicken and lemon.

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium green pepper
  • ½ teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chilli powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 medium onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 200g uncooked long grain rice
  • 280g sweet corn
  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 can chopped tomatoes
  • 1 can chopped tomatoes

    1. Chop the onion and pepper and crush the garlic. Fry onion and pepper and oil in a large skillet over a medium heat for 3 minutes. Add in garlic and fry for a further minute.
    2. Stir in rice, spices and vegetable stock and bring to a boil.
    3. Turn down heat, cover and let simmer for about 15 minutes until rice is cooked through.
    4. Add in all remaining ingredients and cook on a low heat until everything is fully heated.

    • Protein: 2.6g

    • 2 tbsp passata
    • ½ tbsp olive oil
    • ½ tsp dried mixed herbs
    • 50g grated mozzarella
    • 8 slices pepperoni
    • 8 slices jalapeño
    • 2 plain tortillas
    • 1 crushed garlic clove

    1. Heat oven to 200 degrees Celsius.
    2. Combine olive oil, herbs, garlic and passata in a small bowl and season well.
    3. Put tortillas on a lined baking tray.
    4. Spread tomato sauce over the bases and sprinkle over the mozzarella.
    5. Add the jalapeños and pepperoni.
    6. Bake in the oven for 6-8 minutes or until tortilla edges are golden brown and the cheese is melted.

  • 1 onion
  • 1 medium butternut Squash
  • 1 small potato
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 900ml vegetable stock

    1. To prep the veg; dice the onion, peel, and deseed the butternut squash before cutting into wedges, finally peel the potato and cut into 4.
    2. Spray a pan with low-calorie cooking spray and place over a low heat. Add the onion and the crushed garlic and cook for 2 minutes.
    3. Increase to medium heat, add the butternut squash and potato, and cook for a further two minutes. Pour in the stock and simmer for 25 minutes. Season to taste.
    4. To blend use a hand-held blender to blend the mixture until smooth. Serve with some thyme sprinkled on top or any herb of your choice.
