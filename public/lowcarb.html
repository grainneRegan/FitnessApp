Cauliflower Pizza

Bacon and Avocado Frittata

Nutrition
  • (Serves 2)
  • Per serving:
  • Calories: 884
  • Fat: 49g
  • Carbs: 29g
  • Protein: 39g
Nutrition
  • (Serves 4)
  • Per serving:
  • Calories: 467
  • Fat: 38g
  • Carbs: 7g
  • Protein: 22g

  • 60g tomato pasta sauce
  • 60g green pesto
  • 250g cherry tomatoes
  • 100g mozzarella
  • Handful basil leaves
  • 1 cauliflower (about 700g)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 75g ground almonds
  • 30g parmesan
  • 2 eggs

  1. Preheat oven to 140 degrees Celsius.
  2. Roughly chop cauliflower than put in a food processor until very finely chopped.
  3. Put finely chopped cauliflower into clean cloth and squeeze to remove any excess water.
  4. Spread cauliflower out on a lined baking tray and bake for 15 minutes.
  5. Allow to cool and then return the cauliflower to the food processor along with the ground almonds, olive oil, parmesan, eggs and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Blend until a smooth dough is formed.
  7. Turn oven up to 180 degrees Celsius.
  8. Spread dough over lined baking tray to form a circle, about 1 cm in thickness. Bake for about 25 minutes or until golden brown.
  9. Remove from oven and spread tomato sauce and half the pesto over the pizza base. Top with the mozzarella, finely sliced.
  10. Roast at 250 degrees Celsius for 5-10 minutes.
  11. Toss tomatoes, cut into halves, along with the remaining pesto in a bowl and season with pepper.
  12. Top the pizza with the tomatoes and a handful of fresh basil and enjoy!

  • 8 bacon medallions
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 large avocado
  • 2 tsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 ½ tsp dijon mustard
  • 1 small red chilli
  • 200g mixed salad leaves
  • 12 baby plum tomatoes

    1. Fry rashers on high heat until crispy. Roughly chop and set aside.
    2. Warm 1 tbsp oil in the pan. Beat the eggs and then mix in the rashers and season with salt and pepper. Pour the mixture into the pan.
    3. Cook on a low heat for 8 minutes until almost set.
    4. Add the avocado, cut into slices.
    5. Grill for 4 minutes.
    6. Mix the remaining oil, finely chopped chilli, vinegar and mustard in a large bowl. Toss in the salad leaves and the tomatoes cut into halves.
    7. Cut the frittata into wedges and serve along with the salad.

    Courgette lasagne

    Loaded Cauliflower

    Nutrition
    • (Serves 6)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 336
    • Fat: 8.7g
    • Carbs: 28g
    • Protein: 20.3g
    Nutrition
    • (Serves 4)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 289
    • Fat: 24.1g
    • Carbs: 7.1g
    • Protein: 11.4g

    • 500g lean minced beef
    • 2 celery sticks
    • 2 onions
    • 3 garlic cloves
    • 150ml red wine
    • 400g chopped tomatoes
    • 2 large carrots
    • 400ml beef stock
    • 550 ml low fat milk
    • 3 tbsp cornflour
    • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
    • 2 large courgettes
    • 4 vine tomatoes
    • 20g parmesan
    • 40g vintage cheddar
    • 1 tsp black peppercorns
    • ¼ tsp nutmeg

    1. Preheat the oven 180 degrees Celsius.
    2. Fry mince over medium heat for 5 minutes, until golden brown. Finely chop the onions and celery.
    3. Fry half the onions and the celery over a medium heat for 5-6 minutes and season well. Crush the garlic, add to the onions and celery and fry for a further minute. Grate the carrots and add in, along with the red wine. Simmer for 3-4 minutes.
    4. Add the tinned tomatoes, stock and bring to the boil. Cover with lid and turn down heat and let simmer for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove lid and simmer for a further 15 minutes or until sauce has thickened.
    5. Meanwhile, put remaining onions in a separate pan along with the peppercorns and milk (reserving 3 tbsp) and cook over a low heat. Simmer for 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool for 10 minutes.
    6. After cooled, drain with a sieve.
    7. Put cornflour and reserved milk in a small bowl and whisk until a thick paste is formed. Then add to the strained milk and simmer over low heat for 5 minutes. Add the mustard and ground nutmeg and season with salt and pepper
    8. Cut courgette into thin slices lengthways. Blanch in hot water for 1 minute.
    9. Spread half the mince mixture out in a lasagne dish. Layer half the courgette slices on top.
    10. Repeat step 9.
    11. Pour white sauce over top. Slice tomatoes and place on top of white sauce. Grate the cheddar and parmesan cheese and sprinkle on top.
    12. Bake for 30 minutes.
    13. Cut into six slices and serve with a side salad.

  • 500g cauliflower
  • 100g sour cream
  • 2 rashers
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • ¼ teaspoon garlic powder
  • 80g cheddar cheese
  • 2 tbsp chives

    1. Fry rashers on high heat until crispy. Roughly chop and set aside.
    2. Chop cauliflower and add to a microwave safe bowl with 2 tbsp water.
    3. Cover with cling film and microwave for 5-8 minutes until cooked through.
    4. Drain excess water and let cool for a few minutes.
    5. Add cauliflower to a food processor and blend until fluffy.
    6. Add butter, sour cream and garlic powder and blend until smooth.
    7. Add cauliflower mixture to bowl and combine with the chives and half of the cheese and season with salt and pepper.
    8. Top the loaded cauliflower with the remaining cheese and the rashers and microwave for a couple minutes until the cheese is melted.

    Carrot Cake Bars

    Nutrition

    • Carbs: 5g
    • Protein: 16g
    Nutrition
    • (Serves 9)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 235
    • Fat: 12g
    • Carbs: 12g
    • Protein: 10g

  • 450g cod
  • 35g coconut flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 eggs
  • Handful parsley, chopped
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper

    1. Season cod with salt and pepper.
    2. Fry fish with a little oil, over a medium heat for 10 minutes.
    3. After cooked, use a fork to break cod up into small pieces.
    4. Add in the parsley, onions, flour and eggs along with the spices.
    5. Coarsely blend mixture in a food processor.
    6. Compress mixture into fishcakes.
    7. With the remaining oil, fry fish cakes over a medium heat for about 3 minutes on each side.
    8. Serve with a side salad.

    • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 125 ml milk of choice
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 50g granulated sweetener of choice
    • 120g almond butter
    • 60ml sugar free syrup
    • 2 tbsp grated carrots
    • 60 ml pumpkin puree
    • 110g coconut flour

      • For icing:


    • 250g cream cheese
    • 1 tsp cinnamon
    • 2 tbsp granulated sweetener of choice

    1. Line an 8x8 inch baking pan with greaseproof paper.
    2. Combine the flour, protein powder, salt, sweetener and cinnamon in a large mixing bowl.
    3. Mix in the grated carrots and pumpkin puree.
    4. Melt almond butter and syrup over a medium heat until melted. Whisk in the vanilla extract.
    5. Pour into dry ingredients, along with the milk and mix until fully combined.
    6. Transfer into baking pan and spread out evenly.
    7. Refrigerate for 10 minutes.
    8. Combine all ingredients for icing in a mixing bowl and mix until smooth.
    9. Take bars out of fridge and spread icing over the top.
    10. Place bake in the fridge for at least 30 minutes.
    11. Once set cut into 9, evenly sized pieces.

    Szechuan Chicken

    Cheddar Cheese and chive omelette

    Nutrition
    • (Serves 2)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 224
    • Fat: 7g
    • Carbs: 14g
    • Protein: 25g
    Nutrition
    • (Serves 1)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 427
    • Fat: 34.4g
    • Carbs: 3.5g
    • Protein: 25.3g

    • 225g chicken breast
    • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
    • 50g chopped onion
    • ½ tsp sugar
    • ½ tsp ground Szechuan pepper
    • 20g corn starch
    • 2 ½ tbsp soy sauce
    • 1 crushed garlic clove
    • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar

    1. Dice chicken into pieces and toss in large bowl with the corn-starch.
    2. Heat oil in a pan over a medium heat. Add in garlic and chicken and cook until lightly browned
    3. Add soy sauce, sugar and vinegar as well as 2 tbsp of water. Cover and cook for 2 minutes.
    4. Add in the pepper and onions and cook for a further 2 minutes.
    5. Serve immediately.

  • 1 tsp butter
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tbsp chopped chives
  • 34g cheddar cheese

    1. Add the butter to a shallow frying pan and heat over medium heat, allowing the butter to melt.
    2. Meanwhile, whisk three eggs, chives and some seasoning (salt and pepper) in a bowl. Pour into the pan once the butter has completely melted.
    3. Place a lid over the pan and turn down the heat. Once the eggs have started to cook, remove the lid and add the cheese, replace the lid and leave for approx. five minutes.
    4. Remove the lid and ensure the omelette is cooked through.
    5. Fold in half and remove from the pan.

    Baked Tandoori Salmon with cauliflower Rice

    Zucchini Noodles

    Nutrition
    • (Serves 2)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 432
    • Fat: 23g
    • Carbs: 22.1g
    • Protein: 34g
    Nutrition
    • (Serves 4)
    • Per serving:
    • Calories: 197
    • Fat: 14.3g
    • Carbs: 11.2g
    • Protein: g

    • 2 4-ounce salmon fillets
    • 6 ounces plain, sugar-free coconut milk yogurt
    • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
    • 1 tbsp avocado oil
    • 1 tsp ground ginger
    • 1 tsp ground turmeric
    • 1 tsp green cardamom seeds
    • 1 tsp cinnamon
    • 1 tsp cloves
    • 1 tsp cumin seeds
    • For the Cauliflower Rice:
    • 1 medium head of cauliflower, riced
    • 1 tbsp coconut oil
    • 1 spring onion
    • 1 small carrot sliced
    • ¼ cucumber, sliced
    • 1 lime, cut into wedges

    1. In a large bowl, mix the yogurt with all the spices. Add the salmon to the bowl, cover and allow to marinade for 30 minutes.
    2. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F. Line a baking tray with tin foil.
    3. Once the salmon has marinated, place skin side down in the baking tray.
    4. Bake in the oven for 5-10 minutes until a light brown crust has formed.
    5. As the salmon is baking, heat a large saucepan over medium heat and add the coconut oil. Add the cauliflower to the pan and sautee until softened approx. 3 minutes.
    6. Divide the cauliflower and top with the salmon. Serve with the remaining vegetables and some lime juice.

  • 4 medium zucchinis
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp red pepper flakes
  • 50g grated mozzarella
  • 2 chopped medium tomatoes
  • 50g parmesan
  • 1 tsp corn-starch
  • Handful basil leaves
  • 3 crushed garlic cloves

    1. Spiralize zucchini using a spiralizer, or alternatively using a vegetable peeler.
    2. Heat oil in a wok over a medium heat.
    3. Add in garlic, red pepper flakes and zucchini for a few minutes until the zucchini is al dente.
    4. Then add in the tomatoes, parmesan and basil and cook for a further minute.
    5. Enjoy!
